Visual Hierarchy

Company: Amazon

When you first open the page, you're greeted with a few things, all in different sizes and tones. Your eye quickly goes to the large section of white where the featured item is, then it moves up to the rotating banner (movement catches peoples' eyes) and finally ends up at the menus at the top. It directs your attention where it wants you to go (to featured deals and items) without taking away your ability to find menus and search bars.

Fitt's Law

Company: Etsy

When you open Etsy, you're greeted by a grid of large, colorful buttons that link to different sections on the site. These are large, easy-to-read, and easy to click. If you scroll down more, you'll see rows of suggested listings that are also very large and easy-to-click.

White Space and Clean Design

Company: Craigslist

Craigslist is naturally a very simple site, and that works very well for it. Any images are submitted by sellers, so it makes sense to make them stand out more on a plain background. This is also true for the mobile version, it has a very sleek and simple design.
